# karpathy-wiki — Overview

> Top-level synthesis of the entire knowledge base.
> Revised as sources are ingested and understanding deepens.

## Scope

A research wiki built from Andrej Karpathy's X posts, talks, and related source materials, focused on neural networks, LLMs, and deep learning.

## Current State

**1 source ingested.** Karpathy's April 10 X post on the public's divided perception of AI capability.

## Key Themes (so far)

- **Capability is "peaky".** Frontier AI capability is not uniform — it spikes in domains with [[peaky-capability|mechanically verifiable outcomes]] (programming, math, research) and lags elsewhere (writing, advice). See [[peaky-capability]].
- **[[verifiable-rewards|Verifiable rewards]] as the causal lever.** Karpathy's technical story: [[reinforcement-learning]] + machine-checkable rewards = the recent agentic-coding jump. A testable prediction: adjacent domains with good eval harnesses are next.
- **Two audiences, one frontier.** Casual and professional users are both correct about what they observe; they just sample different slices of a peaky capability surface. This produces the perceptual gap Karpathy calls [[ai-psychosis]].
- **Agentic = long-horizon + tool-use + RL.** The products driving the current discourse are [[openai-codex|OpenAI Codex]] and [[claude-code|Claude Code]]. See [[agentic-models]].

## Open Questions

- Is the capability "peak" permanent, or will non-verifiable domains (writing, advice) catch up as reward modeling improves?
- What does the base-rate of Codex / Claude Code success on hour-long agentic tasks actually look like? Karpathy asserts "melting" but gives no numbers.
- Which adjacent domains — theorem proving, sysadmin, data analysis — will inherit the RL-on-verifiable-rewards treatment next?
- Is "AI psychosis" Karpathy's serious coinage or rhetorical? Needs cross-checking against later sources.
