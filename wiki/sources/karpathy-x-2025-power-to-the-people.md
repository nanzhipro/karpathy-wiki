# 2025-04-08 — Power to the People

Karpathy's essay on how LLMs *invert* the usual diffusion path of transformative technology.

## The thesis

Electricity, cryptography, computers, flight, internet, GPS — all diffused **top-down**: government/military → corporations → individuals. LLMs go the other way. ChatGPT hit 400M WAU as a consumer product. Corporations and governments adopt more slowly.

## Why individuals benefit more

1. **Capability shape fits individuals.** LLMs are *quasi-expert across many domains simultaneously* — versatile but shallow and fallible. Individuals are experts in at most one thing; the LLM lets them vibe-code an app, read a legal doc, grok a research paper, do data analytics — things they could not do before. Orgs already employ specialists; the LLM only marginally speeds them up at what they already do.
2. **Org complexity wall.** Corporations deal with integrations, legacy systems, brand guidelines, security protocols, privacy, compliance, legal risk. "You can't just vibe code something. You might be one disastrous hallucination away from losing your job."
3. **Org inertia.** Culture, politics, communication overhead, re-training — all headwinds against rapid adoption of a sparkling-but-fallible tool.

## The "dynamic range" question

Frontier LLM performance is cheap and flat right now: "Bill Gates talks to GPT-4o just like you do. Money can't buy better ChatGPT."

- **Forces that widen dynamic range:** train-time scaling, test-time scaling (thinking), model ensembles.
- **Forces that narrow it:** distillation (small models mimicking big ones).

If dynamic range reopens, the elite can buy more intelligence. "Their child will be tutored by GPT-8-pro-max-high, yours by GPT-6 mini." Today's flatness is unprecedented and possibly temporary.

## Quotable

> "Power to the people. Personally, I love it."

> "Surprise — the future is already here, and it is shockingly distributed."

Nods William Gibson ("the future is already here, it's just not evenly distributed") and flips it.
