# AI Psychosis

Karpathy's term (tentative / rhetorical) for the heightened gravity that professional users of frontier agentic models assign to current capability gains and their downstream repercussions.

## Source

- [Karpathy, AI Capability Gap](../sources/ai-capability-gap.md) — "This group of people is subject to the highest amount of 'AI Psychosis' because the recent improvements in these domains as of this year have been nothing short of staggering."
- [Karpathy, Software Is Changing (Again)](../sources/software-is-changing-again.md) — doesn't use the term "AI psychosis," but the talk operationalizes the split: professional users see the [[partial-autonomy-apps|autonomy slider]] delivering measurable productivity today while casual users see viral failure reels. The talk's emphasis on "keep the human in the loop" is Karpathy's therapeutic response to the psychosis side of the gap, not the failure side.

## Who is affected, per Karpathy

People who **both**:

1. pay for and use frontier agentic models ([[openai-codex|OpenAI Codex]], [[claude-code|Claude Code]]), AND
2. do so professionally in technical domains (programming, math, research)

## Why it exists (Karpathy's theory)

Because capability is [[peaky-capability|peaky]], the professional cohort experiences the peak firsthand (hour-long autonomous code restructuring, vulnerability finding), while the casual cohort experiences only the valleys (Advanced Voice Mode fumbling reels-worthy questions). Both are accurate observations of the *same* frontier; they just describe different slices.

## Tensions / open questions

- Is "psychosis" the right frame? Could it be straightforward accurate perception by those with privileged access, rather than overreaction?
- Karpathy uses the term self-referentially (he is in group 2) — does that suggest he means it seriously or ironically? The 2025 YC talk's repeated insistence on [[iron-man-analogy|Iron Man suit over Iron Man robot]] reads as self-regulation against the psychosis he named.
