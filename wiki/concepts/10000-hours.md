# 10,000 Hours

Karpathy's blunt version of the expertise-threshold rule: serious competence in a technical field requires **roughly 10,000 hours of focused work**, and there is no shortcut the industry has discovered.

## Source

[Karpathy, Berkeley SkyDeck Keynote](../sources/berkeley-ai-hackathon-2024-keynote.md) — delivered to a student hackathon audience.

## The claim stated plainly

- 10,000 hours ≈ 5 years at 40 hours/week, or a decade at part-time pace.
- The hours have to be *focused* — problem-solving, building, debugging — not passive video consumption.
- No LLM, no course, no mentor shortcuts this. Tools change what you can do *per hour*; they don't change the hour count.

## What LLMs change

- Per-hour productivity rises: more artifacts built, more feedback cycles, more bugs fixed.
- Quality of practice rises: instant answers to "why doesn't this compile" unlock hours that would have been wasted.
- The ceiling rises accordingly: 10,000 hours with AI tools produces a more capable person than 10,000 without.

## What LLMs don't change

- The need for deliberate practice.
- The cognitive cost of building accurate mental models.
- The personal identity/ownership that comes from repeated effort on hard problems.

## Karpathy's implicit message to the hackathon crowd

"This technology is real. The opportunity is real. The shortcut is not real. Put in the hours."
