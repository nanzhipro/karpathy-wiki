# Slopacolypse

Karpathy's coinage (Jan 27 2026) for his near-term prediction that 2026 will be the year LLM-generated low-quality content ("slop") floods every major platform.

## The claim

> "I am bracing for 2026 as the year of the slopacolypse across all of github, substack, arxiv, X/instagram, and generally all digital media. We're also going to see a lot more AI hype productivity theater (is that even possible?), on the side of actual, real improvements."

— Karpathy, Jan 27 2026

## Why 2026 specifically

The claim pairs with his [[agentic-engineering|phase-shift]] dating. Now that agents "basically work" for long tasks, the marginal cost of producing plausible artifacts — PRs, blog posts, papers, tweets — collapses. Motivated actors generate at scale.

## His personal response — Jan 31

> "Finding myself going back to RSS/Atom feeds a lot more recently. There's a lot more higher quality longform and a lot less slop intended to provoke. Any product that happens to look a bit different today but that has fundamentally the same incentive structures will eventually converge to the same black hole at the center of gravity well."

**Practical takeaway:** platforms with ad-revenue-sharing or engagement-optimization incentives will all converge on the same slop equilibrium. Formats and protocols (RSS, markdown, gists) with weaker monetization gradients resist longer.

## Second-order effects

- **Training data contamination** — see [[model-collapse]].
- **Verification burden shifts upward** — humans spend more time triaging, less time producing. See [[agentic-engineering]] for the professional-side analog.
- **High-quality signals become more valuable** — taste, curation, and trust networks gain economic weight.
