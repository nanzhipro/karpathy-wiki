# Power to the People

Karpathy's [[karpathy-x-2025-power-to-the-people|Apr 8 2025]] thesis: LLMs *invert* the usual diffusion path of transformative technology — individuals benefit disproportionately compared to organizations.

## The inversion

Transformative technology usually diffuses **top-down** — military/government → corporations → individuals:
- Electricity
- Cryptography
- Computers
- Flight
- Internet
- GPS

LLMs went the other way. ChatGPT hit 400M WAU as a consumer product; corporate and government adoption lagged.

## Three reasons individuals benefit more

1. **Capability shape fits individuals.** LLMs are *quasi-expert across many domains simultaneously* — versatile but shallow and fallible. Individuals are experts in at most one domain; the LLM lets them vibe-code an app, read a legal doc, grok a research paper, do data analytics — things they could not do before. Orgs already employ specialists; the LLM only marginally speeds them up.

2. **Organizational complexity wall.** Corporations deal with integrations, legacy systems, brand guidelines, security protocols, privacy, compliance, legal risk. "You can't just vibe code something. You might be one disastrous hallucination away from losing your job."

3. **Organizational inertia.** Culture, politics, communication overhead, re-training — all headwinds against rapid adoption of a sparkling-but-fallible tool.

## The dynamic-range question

As of 2025, frontier LLM performance is **cheap and flat**:

> "Bill Gates talks to GPT-4o just like you do. Money can't buy better ChatGPT."

Forces that could re-open the range:
- Train-time scaling
- Test-time scaling (thinking)
- Model ensembles

Forces that narrow it:
- Distillation (small models mimicking big ones)

If dynamic range re-opens, the elite can buy more intelligence. *"Their child will be tutored by GPT-8-pro-max-high, yours by GPT-6 mini."* Today's flatness is unprecedented and possibly temporary.

## Quotable

> "Power to the people. Personally, I love it."

> "Surprise — the future is already here, and it is shockingly distributed."

Inverts William Gibson ("the future is already here, it's just not evenly distributed").

## Why this matters

- **Foundational for [[byoai]]** (2026) — personalization as a first-class stance builds on this.
- **Contrasts with the [[software-3-0]] corporate-adoption narrative** — grassroots is where the action is.
- **Explains [[agi-blends-into-2-percent-growth]]** — macro impact lags because orgs are the bottleneck.
- **Explains [[vibe-coding]]'s cultural penetration** — the tech that fits individuals diffuses culturally too.
