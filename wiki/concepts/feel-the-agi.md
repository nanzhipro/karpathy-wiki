# Feel the AGI

Karpathy's framing of a specific qualitative experience developers report after using frontier LLMs for non-trivial work: a *viscerally felt* sense that the thing in front of you is categorically different from prior software. Not a benchmark number — a lived realization.

## Source

[Karpathy, Berkeley SkyDeck Keynote](../sources/berkeley-ai-hackathon-2024-keynote.md) — Karpathy's pitch to the hackathon audience.

## What triggers the feeling

From the keynote and related talks:

- Giving a model a new problem it's never seen and watching it reason cleanly.
- Ad-hoc one-shot code that actually runs.
- Cross-domain transfer: asking about biology and getting a surprisingly coherent answer.
- The moment a model catches an error *you* made.

## Why Karpathy invokes it

- Motivation: if you haven't felt it, you will under-estimate what to build.
- Calibration: people who feel it build bigger; people who don't ship toys.
- Intuition-check against doomer/skeptic takes. The technology is here; timelines debates happen at the margin of what "here" means.

## Relation to timelines

Feeling the AGI doesn't collapse into "AGI is imminent." Karpathy pairs this framing with [[decade-of-agents]] and [[march-of-nines]]: the **capability is present**, the **reliability is not**, and the reliability gap is the decade of work.
