# CS231n

Stanford's *Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition* course, co-founded and taught by Andrej Karpathy in the mid-2010s. One of the most widely-distributed deep learning courses of its era; public lecture notes and video lectures seeded a generation of practitioners.

## Role in Karpathy's arc

- **Teaching lab for pedagogy.** The course's notes and assignments are precursors to the [[ramps-to-knowledge|ramp]] philosophy later crystallized in [[zero-to-hero]] and [[nanogpt]].
- **Distribution.** The notes went viral; for years "CS231n" was how many people first learned convnets in a rigorous way.
- **Transition point.** Karpathy went from Stanford PhD to OpenAI to Tesla while the course was still running; TAs took over and he moved from teacher to visible researcher.
