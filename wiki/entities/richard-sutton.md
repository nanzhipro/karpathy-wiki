# Richard Sutton

RL pioneer, co-author of *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction*, author of "The Bitter Lesson." Invoked by Karpathy in the [[karpathy-x-2025-dwarkesh-recap|Oct 19 2025]] Dwarkesh recap and the [[karpathy-x-2025-rl-and-learning-paradigms|RL posts]].

## Relevance to Karpathy's 2025 arc

Karpathy's [[rl-is-terrible|"RL is terrible but everything else is worse"]] stance directly engages Sutton's tradition:

- **Agrees with Sutton's bitter lesson** — scale and general methods beat hand-engineering.
- **Dissents from Sutton on pure RL as the final path** — Karpathy frames RL as "sucking supervision through a straw," and proposes [[system-prompt-learning]] as the next paradigm Sutton-style RL can't reach.
- **On animals-vs-ghosts** — Sutton's recent Dwarkesh appearance argued LLMs are a dead end because they don't learn like animals. Karpathy counters that we're not trying to build animals — we're [[animals-vs-ghosts|summoning ghosts]], which is a different engineering target.

## Key references

- *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (Sutton & Barto) — the field's textbook.
- "The Bitter Lesson" (2019) — scale and general methods win.
- 2025 Dwarkesh interview — where the LLM-vs-animal disagreement went public.
