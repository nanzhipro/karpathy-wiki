# Zero to Hero

Karpathy's post-Tesla YouTube lecture series on neural networks, from backprop-by-hand through training a GPT from scratch. The screen-captured form of his [[ramps-to-knowledge|ramp]] pedagogy.

## Structure

1. **[[micrograd]]** — autograd from scratch.
2. **Makemore** — character-level language models, bigram through MLP.
3. **[[nanogpt]]** — transformer from scratch, trained on Tiny Shakespeare.
4. **Tokenization** — the BPE deep-dive most courses skip.

Each lecture is multi-hour, code-forward, uninterrupted by marketing or platform filler.

## Why it worked

- The writing happens on-screen: viewers watch the code *being* written, with mistakes.
- Each episode is the artifact you'd build on a good weekend, stretched across the camera.
- The prerequisites are minimal: Python literacy and patience.

## Karpathy's framing in the Berkeley talk

He positions Zero to Hero as the follow-through on a pedagogical instinct he'd had since [[cs231n]]: that the best way to teach a concept is to **watch someone build it in front of you**, not read someone's finished version.
