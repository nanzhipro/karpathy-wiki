# Waymo

**Type:** Company — Google's self-driving subsidiary; operates commercial robotaxi service in SF and other US cities.

## Relevance to this wiki

Karpathy uses Waymo as the benchmark for *how long real deployed autonomy actually takes*:

- **First ride in 2013.** In [[summoning-ghosts-not-animals]], Karpathy recounts a 2013 Waymo ride where "the car drove perfectly — and yet it's 2025 and we still don't have ubiquitous robotaxis."
- **The [[march-of-nines]] exemplar.** Each reliability nine (90% → 99% → 99.9% → ...) is a separate project of equal magnitude. Waymo has been grinding these nines for over a decade and is only now achieving commercial deployment — and only in geofenced, mapped environments.
- **Analogy to coding agents.** In [[software-is-changing-again]] and [[summoning-ghosts-not-animals]], Karpathy argues agentic coding systems face the same march: demos are 90% of the way; the remaining 9%, 0.9%, 0.09% take a decade.
