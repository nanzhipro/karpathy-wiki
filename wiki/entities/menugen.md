# MenuGen

Karpathy's weekend-project demo app: photograph a restaurant menu, get AI-generated images for every dish. Used in the YC talk as the concrete case for the [[vibe-coding]] promise and its hidden costs.

## The anecdote

- Karpathy wrote the actual code in roughly a weekend of vibe coding.
- Shipping it to real users took **a week** of non-coding work: Apple Developer ID, Vercel config, Stripe, Google Auth, domain/SSL.
- The ratio — one weekend of code to a week of DevOps — is the central finding he wants the audience to take.

## Why it's in the talk

- Proves [[vibe-coding]] is real: the idea-to-prototype leap is genuinely faster.
- Proves the bottleneck has moved: the prototype-to-production leap is now the expensive one.
- Motivates [[build-for-agents]]: if the plumbing were agent-navigable, the week would shrink.
