# Tesla Autopilot

Tesla's driver-assistance / self-driving stack. Karpathy was Director of AI at Tesla (2017–2022) and shipped multiple generations of the Autopilot and FSD (Full Self-Driving) software. It is his **deep empirical prior** for everything he says about agents, reliability, and partial autonomy.

## Why it matters to the Karpathy corpus

- The original instance of the [[march-of-nines]]: 12+ years from flawless-demo to still-not-full-autonomy.
- The canonical [[autonomy-slider]] product: Autopilot has graded driver-assist levels with graceful degradation.
- The reason Karpathy defaults to skepticism about agent timelines. He's lived the long-tail.
- **Karpathy still intervenes** when driving an Autopilot-equipped Tesla (per the Dwarkesh interview) — the most honest endorsement of partial autonomy from someone who shipped it.

## Contrasts with [[waymo]]

- **Tesla**: vision-primary, scale to every car, driver in the seat, gradual autonomy.
- **Waymo**: sensor-heavy, geofenced, no driver, full autonomy in its domain.

Karpathy uses both as priors for different aspects: Waymo for "demos aren't deployment," Tesla for "the driver stays in the loop for a long time."
