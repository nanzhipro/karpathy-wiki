# DeepSeek V3.2

The open-weights frontier model from DeepSeek, cited by Karpathy in the Dwarkesh interview as evidence that the gap between open and closed frontier labs has narrowed considerably, and that open-weights releases now materially shape the research landscape.

## Role in the Karpathy corpus

- Invoked as a concrete counterexample to the "only closed frontier labs matter" narrative.
- Used to illustrate that the [[cognitive-core|cognitive-core]] direction is not hypothetical — the models available today are good enough that the question of *how small can it be* is live.

## Relevance to open weights

- Open weights invert parts of the [[llm-os]] analogy: the "CPU" can be downloaded, inspected, and re-hosted.
- Open-weights training runs provide external baselines for the closed-lab reliability arc.
